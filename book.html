Integrative bodywork

What is integrative bodywork?

My integrative bodywork approach draws from a range of modalities including Swedish massage, deep tissue work, BoneWork, assisted stretching, structural integration, trigger point therapy, cupping, and myofascial release. Sessions may include movement, repositioning, or working through clothing depending on the technique. Rather than following a fixed routine, I respond to what your body is presenting in the moment.

I utilize BoneWork very often in my work. Bonework is a subtle yet powerful form of bodywork that brings attention to the body's underlying structure. Rather than focusing only on muscles, this work engages the bones and joints, which are the framework that everything else organizes around. When the body feels supported at this deeper structural level, muscles often release naturally and movement can become easier and more integrated.

30 min cupping + (feet/hands/skull)

~ $35 - $50

Cupping is a treatment method in which a cup or a jar is put onto the skin to cause local congestion through a negative pressure, and then releasing it. In our session, we will use plastic cups and create the negative pressure through a hand pump. Cups will be placed after a short warming massage, and will not be left on for more than 10-15 minutes at a time.

While the cups are on, I invite the option for intentional time for feet, hands, or skull.

The purpose and use of cupping is meant to clear stagnation in the body, which can feel like pain, knots, or congestion, and promote a return to your natural flow.

Cupping contraindications:
  • Blood thinners
  • Bleeding disorder (leukemia, anemia, thrombosis, etc)
  • Broken skin, sunburnt, or ulcerated skin
  • Local edema or directly large blood vessels (including spider veins)
